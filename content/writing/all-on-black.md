## When Hindsight Is a Bitch

There’s a culture war happening right now.

Both sides entrenched. Hateful bile gets spat back and forth.

People decide all kinds of things about you based on which side you're on.

The battle has raged for generations. Shots were fired long before the red pill even existed.

There are those of us who have found connection with both extremes.

Neither side knows what to do with us.

I think you know what I’m talking about.

Cat people.

Versus dog people.

### Screw the pooch

I'd always assumed I was a dog guy.

It makes little sense. One of my earliest memories involves a tiny terrier launching itself at my face.

That experience left me wary around unfamiliar dogs to this day.

But the dogs I knew, I loved.

My Uncle's golden retriever probably did more to bond me to that side of the family than any of my actual cousins.

Then, when I moved to Vancouver in my twenties, a Canadian friend trusted me to look after her Rottweiler while she went home to Ontario.

Yeah. I started with a Rottweiler.

He was nothing like the public relations campaign his breed had received.

Gentle. Affectionate. Even playful.

People would visibly step back when I walked him, convinced I was one loose lead away from a local news story.

Meanwhile he just wanted to chase the flashlight around the house.

When I moved back to the UK I used to tell people I'd love a Rottweiler of my own.

They always assumed I was joking.

Cats, on the other hand, never did much for me.

My sister had two giant black cats. As far as I could tell, they were just serial killers who ignored me.

We coexisted.

I certainly never imagined they would end up teaching me something important.

### Chairman Meow

I started living with cats. Reluctantly at first. A housemate’s tabby here, a girlfriend’s kitten there.

Almost out of habit I found myself searching for a rescue cat after lockdown.

I just wanted some company while I worked from home. Someone who wouldn’t mind if I disappeared on a plane for a couple of weeks.

That was how I met Theo. Equal parts regal and ridiculous.

A black tuxedo with missing teeth and amber eyes that never seem to agree on exactly where they're looking.

Flaws are more interesting.

Theo moved in with me not long after I bought my first house.

I had a lodger in those early days. And wow. She _loved_ cats.

Those two were thick as thieves. That’s when I realised.

Against all odds….

I was _jealous_.

That moulting little chicken thief had gotten under my skin.

### A better bet

It’s not the first time I’ve been wrong.

I almost passed on computer science because I thought my colleagues would be too anti-social for me.

My musical friends tell me they could never work in tech because it isn’t creative.

They have no idea.

We do this constantly.

We decide what careers we'd hate.

What books we'd never read.

What pets we'd never own.

Then we spend years walking past doors we've never actually opened.

Dogs make a strong first impression.

Cats play the long game.

Theo wasn't what I thought I wanted.

Neither was computer science.

But the future has a habit of arriving through doors we almost walk past.
